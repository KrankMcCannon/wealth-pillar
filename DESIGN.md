# Wealth Pillar — Design System

## Brand & Style

Wealth Pillar is a personal & family finance app whose visual identity sits at the intersection of **calm professionalism** and **modern fintech warmth**. The product handles emotionally charged data — salaries, debts, recurring expenses, investments — so the interface deliberately avoids both the sterility of a banking dashboard and the casino-bright glow of crypto products.

The look is built on three convictions:

1. **Warm neutrals over cold greys.** Every neutral is tinted toward a warm yellow-green hue (~90° in OKLCH), so cards, surfaces and borders feel paper-soft rather than steel-cold. The result is a backdrop that feels approachable on a phone at the kitchen table.
2. **A single confident hue carries the brand.** A saturated violet-indigo (`oklch(0.595 0.178 270)`) does almost all the chromatic work — primary actions, navigation highlights, charts, focus rings, key totals. Secondary blue and accent lavender exist only to support; they never compete for attention.
3. **Glass, not glow.** Surfaces lean on `color-mix` translucency and gentle backdrop blur ("liquid glass") rather than neon shadows or bright gradients. Motion is slow and breathy — float, pulse, shimmer — so the app feels considered, never noisy.

The emotional target: *"my finances are under control, and the tool respects my attention."*

## Colors

The palette is authored in **OKLCH** so brightness, chroma and hue compose predictably across light and dark themes. Every token is semantic — components reference roles (`primary`, `destructive`, `surface-card`) rather than raw colors.

### Light theme (default)

- **Canvas.** A warm off-white (`background: oklch(0.94 0.008 90)`) with cards floating slightly brighter (`oklch(0.985 0.003 90)`). The tiny chroma keeps the white from going clinical.
- **Foreground.** Deep warm charcoal (`oklch(0.25 0.015 90)`) — never pure black. Muted text drops to ~45% lightness; subtle/eyebrow text to ~55%.
- **Brand.** Primary violet-indigo (`oklch(0.595 0.178 270)`) is the load-bearing color. Accent (`0.68 0.16 270`) and secondary blue (`0.68 0.08 235`) sit one step softer and exist primarily for tonal hover states and chart variation.
- **Semantic signals.** Destructive is a saturated red-orange (`oklch(0.55 0.22 25)`); warning a goldenrod (`0.75 0.15 85`); success an emerald (`0.7 0.15 150`). These are reserved for state and money direction, never decoration.
- **Borders.** Borders are quiet (`oklch(0.85 0.01 90)`) and frequently expressed as `color-mix(... primary 15%–25%)` against the card to feel tinted rather than dividing.

### Dark theme

Dark mode shifts the hue family from warm-yellow to cool blue-violet (~250°) for the surfaces and lifts the primary's lightness to `0.72` while halving its chroma. The intent is night-time comfort: surfaces feel like velvet midnight rather than pure black, and the primary recedes from "look at me" to "you can find me." Semantic colors (success, destructive, warning) keep their hue but gain lightness; their chroma is reduced ~30% to avoid blooming on OLED displays.

### Finance categorization

A **dedicated category palette** lives alongside the brand colors and is used exclusively for transaction taxonomy (utilities, transportation, health, beauty, food, shopping). Each category gets one or two analogous OKLCH hues that stay perceptually balanced — no category should ever shout louder than the brand primary in a list view.

### Money direction

Positive balances and "safe" budget states are rendered in **primary violet** (not green) on summary surfaces, so the green-success color can be reserved for true confirmations (transfers complete, period closed). Negative balances and overspend always use **destructive red**. Warning amber is reserved for "approaching limit." The triplet is the entire vocabulary of financial state — no fourth color is added.

## Typography

The single typeface is **Spline Sans** (Google Fonts, weights 400 / 500 / 700) with a system-ui fallback stack. Spline Sans was chosen because its slightly humanist proportions feel friendlier than Inter at body sizes, while its geometric numerals stay crisp at the large amounts that dominate the dashboard.

- **Display & headings.** Bold (700) with `-0.025em` tracking, set tight (~`leading-snug`). Page titles use a clamp from ~18px to ~22px so they breathe on tablet without dwarfing mobile.
- **Amounts.** Money is the protagonist of the interface: `amount-md` (20px / 700) for inline rows, `amount-lg` (30px / 700) for hero summaries, both with the same negative tracking as headings. Numerals are `tabular-nums` wherever they appear in lists or charts.
- **Body.** 14px regular at default density, 16px on form inputs (anti-iOS-zoom). Line-height stays at 1.6 globally for legibility on small screens.
- **Labels & eyebrows.** 10–12px medium with optional `0.05em–0.2em` letter-spacing and uppercase, used sparingly to mark sections (e.g., the sidebar's "Workspace" eyebrow). Never used in body text.
- **Tone of weight.** Bold is reserved for headings, amounts, and active nav states; semibold (600) carries subheadings, labels, and primary buttons; regular handles everything else. There is no "extra-bold" or "thin" — the scale stays narrow on purpose.

## Layout & Spacing

The system is built on a **4 px base unit** with a small set of named rhythms rather than a free Tailwind scale. The same module repeats from mobile to desktop:

- **Page padding.** `12px` mobile → `16px` tablet → `24px` desktop. A page never sits flush to the viewport edge.
- **Section gap.** `24px` between major sections (`space-y-6`), `16px` between cards inside a section, `8px` between controls.
- **Card padding.** Four discrete densities — compact (12px), default (16px), comfortable (24px), large (32px). Mobile cards default to compact; dashboard summaries expand to comfortable.
- **Bottom clearance.** Mobile screens always reserve `~80px` at the bottom for the floating navigation plus `env(safe-area-inset-bottom)` so content never disappears behind the home indicator.
- **Containers.** Desktop content caps at `max-w-6xl` and centers; on tablet+ the sidebar (`256px` wide) shifts the page main with `md:pl-64`.

The grid is fluid, not strict: most pages are a single column on mobile and a single wide column with optional auxiliary regions on desktop. Multi-column dashboards are avoided because the personas are not "power analysts" — they're householders glancing at their phone.

## Elevation & Depth

Depth in Wealth Pillar is conveyed by **translucent layering** more than by drop shadows. Three families coexist:

1. **Soft shadows.** All shadows use OKLCH black at 3–8% opacity with widely spread offsets. Even `shadow-xl` is calmer than the Tailwind default. This keeps the warm canvas from looking smudged.
2. **Liquid glass.** The signature surface treatment: `color-mix(in oklch, surface-card 72–88%, transparent)` plus `backdrop-filter: blur(12–20px) saturate(1.1–1.25)` plus a translucent border. Three intensities exist:
   - `liquid-glass-subtle` — the bottom navigation and sticky range bars.
   - `liquid-glass` — modals, popovers, drawers, dashboard cards.
   - `liquid-glass-strong` — focal hero cards and authentication surfaces.
3. **Tinted glass.** Variants tinted with primary / success / warning at ~16% opacity provide *colorful* glass for status callouts (e.g., a "budget exceeded" banner) without resorting to flat fills.

Z-index is governed by a small token set: raised (10) → dropdown (20) → sticky (30) → bottom-nav (40) → sidebar (95) → modal (150) → popover (160) → tooltip (170). Nothing ad-hoc.

## Shapes

The shape language is **rounded-soft, never circular except for status dots and avatars**.

- **Default radius is `1rem`** (16px). It governs cards, buttons larger than 36px, sticky bars, and primary surfaces.
- **Buttons** use `0.75rem` (12px) — visibly tighter than the cards that contain them, which keeps the button from competing with the card's outline.
- **Pills, drawers, and tab lists** use `1.5rem` to feel friendlier (the bottom drawer rounds *only the top corners* at 1.5rem to anchor it to the viewport edge).
- **Inputs** match buttons at `0.75rem` for visual consistency along a form row.
- **Icon containers** are square with `1rem` radius (filled with a subtle 135° gradient mixing 22% → 10% primary), giving them a tactile, "carved-from-the-card" feel.

Strokes are **1px** for borders (occasionally 2px for accent rings on focus). Icon line weight is `1.5–2px` (Lucide defaults), consistent with border weights so iconography feels like the same physical material.

## Motion

Motion is **breathing, not bouncing**. The default duration is 200ms with a soft `cubic-bezier(0.33, 1, 0.68, 1)` easing for hover transforms; standard Material easing (`0.4, 0, 0.2, 1`) for fades.

- **Hover affordances.** `hover-lift` translates -2px and swaps `shadow-md → shadow-lg`. `hover-glass` translates -1px and warms the border toward primary. `morph-card` (used on featured cards) lifts -4px to `shadow-xl`.
- **Ambient motion.** Three slow loops add life without distraction: `liquid-float` (7s vertical drift of ±6px), `liquid-pulse` (5s shadow breath), `liquid-shimmer` (2.8s diagonal sweep at 12% primary). These run on hero cards and loading skeletons only.
- **Onboarding hints.** A `swipe-hint` animation drifts a thumb-glyph right-to-left over 2s to teach the swipe-to-delete gesture on transaction rows.
- **Reduced motion.** `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` removes every ambient loop and disables hover translations. Transitions remain (color/border) but become instantaneous.
- **State changes.** Drawer/modal entry uses `slide-in-from-bottom` (mobile) or `zoom-in-95 + fade-in` (desktop) at 200–300ms.

## Components

### Cards

Cards are the primary container. They default to a soft surface (`bg-card` + 1px tinted border + `shadow-sm`) with `1rem` radius and `16px` padding. Three variants:

- **Soft card** — neutral, used for transaction rows and list items.
- **Elevated card** — slightly stronger primary-tinted border and `shadow-md`; used for budget summaries and hero metrics.
- **Glass card** — backdrop-blurred, used for dashboard summaries that float over a tinted hero region.

### Buttons & Actions

Buttons are 36px tall (default), 32px small, 40px large, with 12px radius and a 16px gap to icons. The system distinguishes six variants (default, secondary, outline, ghost, destructive, cancel). The floating action button on dashboards is a 44px circular button filled with primary, used for the single most important create-action on a page (e.g., "Add transaction").

Focus rings are universal: `2px` ring at `30%` primary opacity, plus a darker primary border. There is no separate "active" outline — focus and active share the same affordance.

### Inputs & Forms

Inputs match buttons (12px radius, 1px primary-tinted border). They use the dedicated `surface-input` color which is one step brighter than the card to read clearly inside a card. Placeholder text drops to 40% foreground opacity. Selection swaps to primary fill. Validation errors swap the border to `destructive` and add a same-color helper line beneath.

### Navigation

Two navigation chassis coexist:

- **Mobile bottom navigation.** Translucent glass bar with safe-area padding, 4–5 icon labels, the active item filled with `primary` at full opacity. Always reachable by thumb.
- **Desktop sidebar.** Fixed 256px column with a "Workspace / Wealth Pillar" eyebrow brand, 44px-tall pill nav items, and a settings link in the footer. Active items use `sidebar-primary` (matched to the brand violet) with white text. Sidebar disappears on auth and onboarding routes.

A sticky page header sits above the content on every authenticated route, glassy and `56px` tall, carrying back, title/subtitle, and contextual actions.

### Status & Indicators

- **Budget status** is encoded with a 2px colored dot (safe = primary, warning = warning, danger = destructive) plus a percentage in matching color. The progress bar's fill follows the same color rule.
- **Category badges** render the category icon over a tinted square (color at 15% opacity background, full color foreground). Categories are *never* differentiated by shape.
- **Toasts** appear top-right (desktop) or top-center (mobile) as glass cards with a leading colored bar (primary / success / warning / destructive).

### Charts

Charts (line, bar, pie) draw exclusively from the `chart-1…chart-5` ramp — five tonally related violet shades. Reading order is enforced by tone, not hue, so colorblind viewers still see hierarchy. Grid lines use the border token at low opacity. Hover dots and tooltips reuse the popover surface.

### Skeletons & Empty States

Skeletons are shaped like the content they replace, filled with `primary @ 10–15%` and animated with `pulse` (or `liquid-shimmer` on hero cards). Empty states use a 64px circular icon container in `primary @ 10%`, a heading, a single prose line of guidance, and one optional primary action — never a wall of help text.

## Accessibility & Inclusivity

- All semantic color pairs (foreground on surface, primary-foreground on primary, etc.) target WCAG AA contrast in both themes.
- Interactive targets are `≥44×44px` on mobile (icon buttons, drawer summaries, nav items).
- Focus rings are always visible and never removed; the ring color is the same in both themes.
- Reduced motion preferences disable ambient animations and hover transforms but preserve color/state transitions.
- Skip links and ARIA-current are wired into the dashboard layout so keyboard / screen-reader users land on the main content past the sticky header.

## Design Voice

When a decision is unclear, choose the option that feels more like:

- **Calm**, not energetic.
- **Tinted**, not neutral.
- **Translucent**, not opaque.
- **One bold accent**, not three.
- **Numerals first**, decoration last.
- **Quiet borders**, soft shadows.

The product earns trust by being legible, considered, and a little bit warm. Every token in this file exists to defend that intent.
